About me

The first thing is to recognise that there is nothing wrong with not being completely sure of yourself. People are pressured by society to always
“be sure of yourself” or “be confident”, as if there was a switch we could turn on by choice. So don’t be too hard on yourself for example being
socially awkward or not knowing your weaknesses and strengths. Your woes are shared by all.

In general, I would say reflection of oneself with respect to life’s experiences, good or bad, promote growth. Treasure all the experiences life has
presented to you. From experience, I can say the quickest way to finding out more about yourself is to put oneself into situations out of your comfort zone.
Take up that job offered in a country you know nothing about, go to that event you so dread, learn a new language etc. Whenever you are put on a spot,
the essence of who you are bubbles up to the surface and you get added clarity about your person. Notice how I said this is a good way to go about knowing
yourself but it isn’t the only way.
